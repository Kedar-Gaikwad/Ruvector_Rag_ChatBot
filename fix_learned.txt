right now the challenge i am facing is : Docling’s DocumentConverter is highly optimized for analyzing the visual structure of static documents (like research papers, articles, and reports). It uses AI layout models to identify paragraphs, headings, and tables in the base text layer of a document.However, fillable PDFs (like an IRS W-9) are built differently:The Base Layer: This contains the static, printed text of the document (e.g., "1 Name of entity/individual").  The Interactive Layer (AcroForms/XFA): When you type "Sai Vishnu Enterprise" or "halifax" into a PDF form, that text is stored in floating "form fields" that sit on top of the base document as annotations.By default, Docling is reading the base template and converting it into a clean Markdown list (which is why your chunk output shows perfectly formatted bullet points like - 1 Name of entity/individual...), but it is bypassing the interactive form fields entirely.How to Fix ItIf you want Docling to chunk the filled-in values alongside the standard text, you need to merge those layers together.Option 1: "Flatten" the PDF (Recommended & Easiest)You need to convert the interactive PDF into a standard, static ("flat") PDF before feeding it to Docling.Open your filled new_w-9.pdf in Chrome, Edge, or any standard PDF viewer.Select Print.Instead of a physical printer, choose "Print to PDF" or "Save as PDF" as your destination.Save this new file.This process "burns" your form inputs directly into the base text layer. If you upload this new flattened PDF to your Colab script, Docling will read "Sai Vishnu Enterprise" and "halifax" exactly as if they were printed text.Option 2: Use a Form-Specific Extraction ToolIf your primary goal is to extract data from filled forms (rather than doing semantic chunking for a RAG application), Docling is actually the wrong tool for the job.Instead of a structural parser, you should use a library designed to read PDF dictionaries and annotations. For example, the pypdf or PyMuPDF (fitz) libraries in Python can instantly pull exact values from form fields without needing AI layout models.
